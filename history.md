# History of Aern

The history of Aern is long and marked by great leaps forward followed by devastating falls. It is a world shaped as much by the gods as by the people who live in it — and the consequences of what happens when those two forces stop working together.

The ages of Aern are broadly understood as follows:

| Age | Key Events |
|-----|------------|
| The Age of Guidance | Gods walk among mortals, heroes and legends |
| The Age of Expansion | The Steel Age, the Azzurium Age, the rise of technomancy |
| The Convergence | The gods withdraw, the continents collide, the monsters arrive |
| The Age of Embers | A world rebuilding from the ashes |
| The Kindling | The Estasi slowly return, civilisation reforms |
| The Age of Ascension | Unknown — the next great leap |

---

## The Age of Guidance

In the earliest days of Aern, the world was young and the [Estasi](/estasi) were present in it. Vivere had built the world and filled it with life, and the other gods walked among their people, teaching them, guiding them, and sometimes intervening directly in mortal affairs.

This was a time of heroes. The Estasi would often take a particular interest in an individual, becoming their patron deity and granting them a connection to divine magic. These figures became legends — some of them still remembered in folk tales and religious texts long after the age itself had passed.

The gods were not always gentle. They had their disputes, their favourites, their blind spots. But their presence was a stabilising force, and the world of Aern grew under their watch.

## The Age of Expansion

### The Steel Age

As the generations passed, the people of Aern became more capable. They built cities, developed trade routes, and began to understand the natural world in ways that required less direct guidance from the Estasi. Towns grew into cities. Continents became connected. Iron was worked, ships were built, and the world shrank.

The Estasi watched with pride and, at times, concern. They began to step back — not fully, but enough for humanity to feel the increased independence. The gods remained present, still answering prayers, still appearing in times of great need, but the age of daily divine intervention was beginning to close.

### The Azzurium Age

The Steel Age gave way to something more dangerous — the Azzurium Age, a period of rapid magical and technological experimentation that would eventually bring the world to its knees.

The catalyst was azzurium, the blue mineral found on [Azzura](/azzura), the moon of Aern. Researchers discovered that azzurium could store magical energy and that, with the right knowledge, it could be used to pull that energy from [Inizio](/inizio) itself — without needing to be granted power by the gods. This was the birth of technomancy, and it spread quickly.

The Estasi were deeply troubled by this. In the past, divine magic had been a gift — a connection between god and mortal, offered willingly. Technomancy circumvented this entirely. It was not stolen, exactly, but it was taken without permission, and the gods began to feel the effects of it as the ambient magic of the world was increasingly drawn into gems and machines.

The Estasi withdrew further. They did not act immediately, but their presence in the mortal planes became increasingly rare. Prayers went less answered. Patron relationships faded. The age of heroes was truly over.

### The Towers

The final act of the Azzurium Age was the construction of two great towers, one on each of Aern's two continents. They were the work of a now-infamous technomancer whose name has been largely scrubbed from history, though their legacy could not be more permanent.

The towers were designed to reach closer to [Azzura](/azzura) — to the moon where the Estasi were said to dwell — with the intention of drawing more divine power directly into the azzurium gems. It was a flawed technique built on a fundamental misunderstanding of the divine power. However, the research conducted in this tower was real and the connection to [Inizio](/inizio) was pulled closer.

The towers had pulled too hard on things not understood, literally pulling the continents they sit on toward each other. They had opened connections not only to [Inizio](/inizio) but also to other planes. They had opened gateways for influences of other worlds to spill out into this one.

## The Convergence

The Convergence is the name given to the calamity caused by the research of these towers. A period of roughly a century during which the world was permanently reshaped.

### The Withdrawal of the Estasi

The Convergence refers to the different worlds coming together. During this same time the Estasi made their choice to withdraw their presence from Aern.

The effects were felt in ways that most people didn't immediately understand. Weather patterns became more extreme. The land itself became less predictable. Ancient protections — the kind no one had thought to question because they had always simply been there — began to fail.

### The Collision

The most dramatic consequence of the Convergence was geological. The magical forces that had held the two great continents in balance — forces connected, though few knew it, to the presence of the Estasi — gave way. Over decades, the continents shifted. Then, in what survivors described as a generation of slow-motion disaster, they collided.

The impact threw up a vast mountain range at the point of collision, a wall of stone that divided the resulting supercontinent in two. On one side, the remnants of the old civilisations survived, damaged but intact enough to eventually rebuild. On the other, the land was broken and wild — reshaped not just by the physical impact but by the planar tears left behind by the towers, one of which still stands near the center of this space.

### The Arrival

The towers had torn holes between worlds. Through those holes came things that did not belong on Aern. Creatures from other planes of existence — some merely unfamiliar, others genuinely monstrous — flooded through before the tears slowly closed over the following decades. Many of them settled in the wild lands beyond the mountains, which adds to the danger of this side of the continent.

Not everything that came through was hostile. Some of those displaced found a place on Aern, and their descendants are part of the world that exists today.

## The Age of Embers

The technologies of the Azzurium Age are gone. Some remnants survive — old machines, fragments of azzurium-worked devices, ruins that hint at what was once possible — but the knowledge to build or repair them has been lost. The world is older-feeling than the age that preceded it, more like the ancient days of sword and sorcery than the industrialised world that came before the Convergence.

Magic still exists, but it is wilder. Without the Estasi actively managing the flow of divine power, magic is less predictable and less understood. Those who can use it are respected and feared, often at the same time.

The great mountain range that formed during the Convergence — sometimes called the Dividing Mountains or simply the Ridge — marks a hard boundary in daily life. Civilisation exists on one side. The wilder lands beyond are dangerous, home to the creatures that came through the planar tears and to whatever has grown from them in the centuries since.

The Estasi have not returned, not fully. But there are signs. Prayers that seem to be answered. Patron connections that form in ways that haven't been seen since the Age of Guidance. Their severed connection to Aern difficult to restore, but the distance is not absolute.

*Most stories set on Aern take place in the Age of Embers.*

---

## The Kindling
 
The Kindling is a time of cautious hope. The Estasi are beginning to return in earnest, and with them comes a slow restoration of the divine connection that was severed during the Convergence. Civilisation is rebuilding, old knowledge is being rediscovered, and the balance between the wild lands and the settled world is beginning to shift.
 
It will not be the Age of Guidance reborn. The world is too changed for that, and the gods are too changed by the separation. But it is a new age of possibility, and those who live through it will help decide what Aern becomes next.
 
---
 
## The Age of Ascension
 
The Age of Ascension is the distant horizon of Aern's history — an era when the world looks outward. The connections to other planes opened during the Convergence, once a source of catastrophe, have become a source of understanding. Planar travel becomes possible. Then, in time, something further still.
 
The stars. Other worlds. What lies beyond Aern entirely.
 
The shape this takes is still being written.
