# The Wildlands

Beyond the mountains lies a land that does not welcome visitors. The eastern side of the supercontinent — what was once a separate world entirely, before the Convergence reshaped everything — is dangerous, largely unsettled, and strange in ways that are difficult to prepare for.

Most people on Thren call it the Wildlands. The people who actually live there do not use this name.

## The Land

The Wildlands are not simply wilderness. They are a place where the boundary between Aern and other planes of existence has never fully healed. The Convergence tore holes between worlds, and though most of those tears have closed, the land itself remembers them. Creatures from other planes settled here in the centuries since. Plants grow that have no business growing on Aern. Weather patterns behave according to rules that do not always match the season or the geography.

Travellers crossing the mountains into this territory do so aware that the rules they are used to may not apply.

## The Tribes

The Wildlands are not empty. The tribes who have lived here across generations — nomadic peoples who predate the Convergence and the Highwall and the name the western continent gave to their home — remain. They have adapted to the danger rather than fled from it. Their knowledge of the terrain, including its stranger properties, is considerable. It is not freely shared with outsiders.

Tribal culture is broadly consistent across the different groups, rooted in movement and accumulated knowledge rather than fixed settlement. Their camps shift with the seasons and the land. Some have names that have filtered through to western maps — Newlight among them, a summer camp whose name echoes an older one — but most remain unknown outside the tribes themselves.

## The Sacred Tree

At what was once the centre of the old continent stands a tree unlike anything else on Aern. It is vast — visible from a distance that should make it impossible to see — and the forest that has grown around it over the centuries is unlike the surrounding land. The trees here are wrong in ways that are hard to articulate. The light behaves differently. The sounds are not the sounds of an Aernian forest.

The tribes hold the tree as sacred. They do not approach it casually. The forest around it is dangerous enough that most keep a respectful distance, venerating it from afar.

One tribe has learned to live within that forest. They know paths through it that others cannot find, and they make their camps in the shadow of the tree itself. What they know about it, they have not chosen to share.

## Outsiders

Travellers from Thren occasionally cross to the wildlands — scholars, adventurers, those with something to prove or nowhere else to go. Some return. Those who do rarely describe the experience in terms that make it sound worth repeating, though this does not appear to discourage the next group.

The locals tend to avoid Eastgate, where the outsiders enter the wildlands. This area has a couple smaller guard posts to defend the gate itself.
