# Trademoor

Trademoor is the capital of Thren and the seat of the merchants guild. It sits on the Eastern shore of the Stillmere, where the Drayrun empties into the inland sea, and it has stood in some form on this ground longer than most of its residents can reliably account for.

The city bears the marks of difficult centuries. Roughly a third of its original area was lost to the Stillmere during the upheaval of the Convergence, and what remains has been rebuilt to varying degrees of success. The market streets at its core are largely intact. The outer districts are rougher, some of them rebuilt from the ground up in the generations since, the southern most sections are now bellow the water level.

## The Merchants Guild

Trademoor is governed by the merchants guild, as it has been since before the guild had a name for what it was doing. The guild began as a practical body — merchants settling disputes, managing supply, making the market work — and became a government by degree rather than declaration.

Guild leadership is determined by challenge. A candidate declares themselves by stating, publicly, that they could do the job better than whoever currently holds it. This is then argued about. There is no fixed schedule, no formal ballot. The argument is the election, and always has been.

The guild's claim to govern Thren rests on continuity. Trademoor is the capital. It has always been the capital. Whatever the Convergence took, it did not take that, and the guild holds that legitimate authority over the continent flows from this fact as it always has.

## Layout

The market streets are the heart of the city — a wide main thoroughfare with a grid of alleys running off it. The guild hall overlooks the market from above. Housing spreads outward from the commercial core in all directions, maintained and orderly close to the centre, rougher and more improvised toward the edges.

Raised cart roads run above the residential streets, built from older structures that have been repurposed over the years. Some of the mechanisms embedded in them still function, though few people alive fully understand them.

The Drayrun runs along the city's edge and empties into the Stillmere nearby. Its ports see some traffic, though less than the city's size might suggest.

### The Market Streets

The main street and its alleys hold the bulk of Trademoor's commerce. Tailors, bakers, confectioners, barbers, jewellers, alchemists, herbalists, and others occupy the storefronts along the main thoroughfare. Armourers and weapon smiths work further in, clustered in a side alley locals call Hot Alley. Grain sellers, jerky stands, and potters fill the gaps.

The market has a rhythm to it that the rest of the city organises itself around.

## The Political Situation

Trademoor's authority over Thren is contested. [Dragonstail Reach](/embers/locations/thren/dragonstail), to the south, has grown in the centuries since the Convergence into a significant power in its own right, and its ruling family disputes the guild's claim to continental governance.

The conflict has not become open war, but it has never fully cooled. Skirmishes occur at the margins. Each side works to secure the loyalty of the settlements between them. Trademoor's position is straightforward: the capital is the capital, the guild has governed since before the Convergence, and no amount of southern ambition changes what the city is and what that means.
