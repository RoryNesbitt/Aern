# Ashspire

The temple of [Dati](/deities/estasi#dati) has stood at the centre of Ashspire for longer than the Convergence, longer than the empire that surrounded it, longer than most of the institutions that once considered themselves its peers. It stands still.

The knowledge accumulated within its walls survived intact. The towers survived intact. The priests who maintained both through the worst of what the Convergence brought did so without outside support, because Ashspire has never required outside support. The work continued because the work always continues.

## What Ashspire Offers

More pre-Convergence knowledge exists within Ashspire's walls than anywhere else on Thren. This is not an accident — it is the result of centuries of careful accumulation by an institution that understood, long before the Convergence made it obvious, that knowledge is the thing most worth preserving.

Students who come to Ashspire work alongside researchers engaged with that knowledge directly — not as historical curiosity, but as a living foundation for understanding what the world was, what it is, and what it might become. The standard is high. The access to material unavailable elsewhere is unmatched.

Those who study here study under [Dati](/deities/estasi#dati). The pursuit of knowledge is devotion at Ashspire, and always has been. Students who arrive expecting to separate the two find, usually within their first season, that the distinction does not hold.

## The Towers

Seven towers rise above the temple grounds, as they always have. The research conducted within them draws on Ashspire's accumulated knowledge of the Azzurium Age — its methods, its discoveries, and the questions it raised that the Convergence interrupted before they could be answered.

Tower assignment remains the senior priest's judgement alone. It is the highest distinction Ashspire offers, and it is not given lightly. Those who receive it tend to produce work that justifies the decision.

## Admission

The temple accepts students on the basis of aptitude and genuine intent. There is no imperial admission process, no quota, no preference given on the basis of origin or connection. The council of senior priests considers each application seriously and decides accordingly.

Ashspire is self-sustaining and politically independent. It has declined, with consistent courtesy, to align itself with any faction in the current political situation on Thren. Students of all backgrounds and allegiances study here without difficulty. What they do with what they learn, once they leave, is their own affair.

## Getting Here

Ashspire is accessible from Trademoor by lightning train. The towers are visible on the approach.
