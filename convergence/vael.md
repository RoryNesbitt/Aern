# Vael

When the consequences of the towers begin to manifest, Aldric Vael does not stop.

## The First Signs

The early warnings are subtle enough that reasonable people disagree about them. Weather patterns shifting. The ambient magic of the world behaving inconsistently. Prayers going unanswered in ways that could be coincidence. Aldric is aware of these changes. His notes from this period acknowledge them directly, recorded with the same careful attention he brings to everything.

His conclusion is that the towers are working. That what looks like disruption is the natural friction of pushing into territory that has never been studied before. That the solution to unexpected results is more data, not less.

He adjusts his methods. He continues.

## The Escalation

As the signs become harder to dismiss — the continents shifting, the Estasi withdrawing, the tears between planes beginning to open — Aldric's notes become something more complicated. The certainty does not leave them. But something else enters: a quality that those who have since read the surviving fragments describe differently depending on their own disposition. Some call it denial. Some call it focus. Some call it the particular courage, or particular blindness, of a man who has built his entire life on a belief and cannot afford to find out he was wrong.

He believes he can correct it. That the damage is a variable he had not accounted for, not a sign that the work itself was flawed. He pushes the towers harder, attempting to use the same mechanisms that caused the disruption to reverse it.

The world gets worse.

## The End of the Record

Vael's notes stop before the Convergence reaches its worst. Whether this is because he stopped writing, because the records were lost, or because something else happened entirely, cannot be determined from what survives.

He is not found. No colleague reports a final conversation. The tower on Ferrath still stands amid a land that is reshaping itself around it. The tower on Thren still stands as the ground slowly raises around it.

The empire that might have demanded answers from him does not survive long enough to ask.
