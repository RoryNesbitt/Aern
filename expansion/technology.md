# Technology of the Azzurium Age

The Azzurium Age takes its name from azzurium — the blue mineral found on [Azzura](/azzura), Aern's moon — but the technology that defines it runs on something older and stranger than any mineral. It runs on divine power, pulled directly from the plane of the gods and put to work in engines, tools, and machines that most people use without thinking too hard about what is actually inside them.

## Arcane Power

Magic has always existed on Aern. A rare few people are born with a natural connection to it, capable of drawing on it without tools or training. For most of history this was simply a fact of the world — some people could do things others couldn't, and that was the end of it.

Technomancers changed this. Not by making magic available to everyone in the old sense, but by finding a way to capture it, store it, and put it inside objects that anyone could use. The breakthrough that made this possible was the discovery that azzurium — raw or refined — could hold arcane energy like a vessel holds water. Fill a gem with power, seal it correctly, and it becomes a portable source of energy that can drive a machine indefinitely, or until it runs dry.

The purple sparks that characterise arcane power in use are not decorative. They are the visible signature of energy being drawn from another plane into this one — a small, controlled version of the same process that occurs naturally when lightning strikes. Technomancers learned to induce this process deliberately, pulling power from the air itself and directing it where it was needed.

Anyone can learn technomancy given sufficient study. It requires patience and precision more than any innate ability, and Ashspire produces trained technomancers in considerable numbers. The craft ranges from the relatively simple — charging a gem, building a basic arcane circuit — to work of significant complexity that takes years to master.

## Azzurium Gems

The foundation of arcane technology is the Azzurium gem — a refined crystal capable of storing arcane energy and releasing it in a controlled manner. In practical terms it functions like a battery: charged, installed, and depleted over time depending on what it is powering.

Charging a gem requires drawing arcane lightning — the purple sparks of divine energy — into the crystal through a purpose-built rod. The process is well understood and widely practiced. Gem charging stations exist in every city and most large towns, and the trade in charged gems is one of the more reliable businesses in the empire.

Arcane gems power most of the technology that defines city life in the Azzurium Age. Tools, lighting, workshop machinery, smaller vehicles — anything that requires a consistent and portable power source. In cities, residents interact with gem-powered devices as a matter of daily routine. In rural settlements, the same technology exists but is less common, and a charged gem is a more considered purchase.

## Lightning Trains

The lightning trains are the most significant application of arcane power in the empire, and the most visible. They connect Trademoor to the wider continent, moving passengers and freight at speeds that would have seemed implausible a century before.

The trains cannot run on gems. The power required to move them at speed is too great for any practical number of stored gems to sustain. Instead, the engines contain their own generators — arcane mechanisms that pull energy directly from their surroundings as the train moves, converting it continuously into the power needed to maintain speed.

The result is the characteristic appearance of a train in motion: purple-white sparks crackling constantly from the rods mounted above the engines, the air around them alive with drawn power. The sound — a deep, irregular crackling that builds as a train approaches — is recognisable from some distance. In cities where trains run regularly it becomes part of the background, noticed only by those new to it or those who grew up somewhere quieter.

Because the generators pull energy from their immediate surroundings, a train in motion near a settlement poses a risk to anything arcane in its path. For this reason, train tracks are elevated when they pass through or near populated areas, running above the streets on raised stone and ironwork. Where a settlement grew up around an existing track rather than the other way around, exceptions exist — and locals learn to account for them.

## Power Lifters

Heavy industrial work across the empire is assisted by Power Lifters — large mechanical suits worn by a single operator, providing enhanced strength and telekinetic capability for moving loads that would otherwise require cranes or large teams.

A Power Lifter encases the operator from the waist up, with mechanical arms that extend and amplify the wearer's movements. The arcane component allows it to grip and manipulate objects without direct physical contact — the operator positions the arms, and the machine does the lifting. They are common in dockyards, construction sites, warehouses, and anywhere that large quantities of heavy material need to be moved efficiently.

## Mini Lifters

Where a Power Lifter moves bulk, a Mini Lifter works with precision. The name undersells the equipment — a Mini Lifter is a substantial piece of kit, consisting of a large articulated gauntlet worn on the submissive hand, connected by cable to a backpack housing the arcane gem and the mechanisms that power it. The other hand wears a smaller, intricate control glove through which the operator can manipulate the lifted object to position it where it is needed.

The division of labour between the two hands is deliberate. The gauntlet lifts. The glove places. An experienced operator can manipulate objects in the air with considerable accuracy, making Mini Lifters useful for work that requires both strength and care — delicate assembly, precise installation, handling materials that cannot be dropped.

They are common in workshops, arcane engineering facilities, and anywhere that combines heavy components with work that requires a steady hand. Some variations of these exist which replaces the backpack with a large stand for parking the gauntlet and keeping an item held while the user detatches themselves.

## Blimps

Arcane blimps fill the skies above the empire's cities — large dirigibles kept aloft by a combination of lifting gas and arcane assistance, their engines powered by gems. They carry passengers, freight, and in some cases serve as mobile platforms for work that needs to be done at height.

A city skyline in the Azzurium Age includes blimps as a matter of course — moored at towers above the rooftops, moving slowly between destinations, or holding position while work is conducted below. They are slower than the trains and more expensive to operate, but they go places the tracks do not.

## A Note on Magic

The existence of technomancy has not made natural magic obsolete or irrelevant — only less central to daily life than it once was. Those with an innate connection to arcane power still exist, and what they can do remains distinct from what a technomancer with a gem can achieve. The relationship between the two is not always comfortable. Magic was feared before it was understood, and the understanding that technomancy brought has not entirely displaced the fear.

Vael's finding — that the power behind all of this is divine in nature — is accepted as established fact in academic circles. Its implications are discussed rather than acted upon. Most people who use arcane technology daily do not think about where the power comes from, any more than they think about what holds the moon in the sky.
