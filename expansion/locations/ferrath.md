# Ferrath

Ferrath is the second continent of Aern — smaller than Thren, largely uncolonised, and known among those who have studied such things for its unusual closeness to other planes of existence. The empire has made no serious effort to settle it. The people who live here did not ask them to.

## The Land

Ferrath is wild in a way that Thren has long since forgotten how to be. Its interior is dense and difficult, its coasts unwelcoming to large ships. There are no cities here, no roads built to last, no infrastructure that assumes permanence. The continent belongs to those who move with it rather than those who try to hold it still.

## The Tribes

Ferrath is home to multiple distinct tribes, broadly similar in culture despite the distances between them. They are nomadic peoples, moving between seasonal camps rather than building permanent settlements. Their knowledge of the land is deep — accumulated across generations of reading terrain, weather, and the stranger signals that Ferrath produces in abundance.

Known camps include Lightreach and Coldbreak, summer and winter camps used by the same tribe, their names reflecting the conditions of each season rather than any fixed geography.

The tribes have their own names for the world around them. Thren, to the west, they call the Settled Shore — a name that carries no particular warmth. Ferrath itself they know by older names that do not translate cleanly, rooted in a relationship with the land that does not have an equivalent in the imperial tongue.

## Planar Closeness

Ferrathans have always known that their land is strange. The stories that pass between camps describe worlds of brilliant colour and worlds where nothing lives at all. People vanish near certain areas for years, or return several decades older than they should be. The details are often debated. The disappearances are no, neither are the people reappearing a few months later but several decades older.

Most tribes treat these areas as sacred and keep their distance.

## The Tower

At the centre of Ferrath, atop a vast vein of raw inizium, stands the second tower of [Vael](/expansion/vael). The tribes had already avoided this ground — it had been considered sacred and unsettling long before the tower was built. After it was erected, they avoided it completely.

The tower was built to take advantage of Ferrath's closeness to other planes, drawing that connection into Vael's research. What it ultimately drew in was considerably more than intended.
