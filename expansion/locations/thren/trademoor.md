# Trademoor

Trademoor is the capital of the Empire and the largest city on Thren. It sits on the western shore of the Stillmere, where the Drayrun completes its journey across the continent and empties into the inland sea. The city grew the way most great cities do — not by design, but because people kept arriving and nobody asked them to leave.

## Origins

There was no founding moment for Trademoor. A market appeared on the coast of the Stillmere where the mouth of the Drayrun made it easy to bring goods in by water and move them inland. The market grew. Housing followed the market. More merchants followed the housing. The city expanded outward from its original trading streets, with the wealthiest residents staying close to the commerce that made the city worth living in, and the poorest settling at the edges where the ground was cheaper.

The settlements that grew up around Trademoor — a short distance outside the city proper — contributed to its expansion without ever quite becoming part of it.

## The Merchant Parliament

Trademoor was never formally governed. It organised itself.

The merchants guild was already meeting at the end of each trading day to settle disputes, agree prices, and manage supply. Over time these meetings became something more structured, and the guild found itself making decisions that affected the city as much as the market. Nobody declared this a parliament. It simply became one.

Elections were not held on a schedule. A candidate declared themselves by announcing — usually to a crowd, sometimes to the sitting guild head directly — that they could do the job better. This was then argued about. The argument was the election.

The invention of taxation changed the character of these meetings. The guild became something more like a government, though it never fully shed the instincts of the market. Commerce and governance happened in the same building, often on the same day.

## Layout

The market is the heart of the city, built along a wide main street with several grid-like alleys running off it. Parliament sits above the market, looking down over the trade it grew from.

Housing spreads outward in all directions from the commercial core. The closer to the market, the better maintained. The further out, the more the city trails off into something rougher.

The Drayrun passes along the city's edge on its way into the Stillmere and is used for deliveries and smaller passenger travel by boat. The more significant arteries are the lightning train lines — raised tracks that run above the streets, homes, and shops, connecting Trademoor to the wider empire. The trains travel fast enough that their lightning rods pull constant sparks as they move, and the crackling sound of an approaching train is as much a part of city life as the noise of the market below.

The train station sits beside the parliament building. Guild members step directly inside. Everyone else descends to street level.

### The Market Streets

The main street runs through the commercial core, with alleys branching off in a rough grid. A typical stretch might pass through tailors, bakers, confectioners, barbers, jewellers, alchemists, a music shop, and herbalists. Armourers and weapon smiths cluster together in one of the side alleys — known locally as Hot Alley for the heat that pours out of it at all hours. Grain sellers, jerky stands, and potters are scattered throughout without much logic to their placement.

The market shifts. Stalls appear and disappear. What is true one season may not be true the next.

## Power

The energy that powers arcane machines across the empire — including the lightning trains — is divine magic, drawn from the power of the gods as it spills from their plane into this one.

Artificers developed a method of capturing this energy by forcing a lightning strike into a purpose-built rod, using the resulting charge to fill arcane gems. These gems power a wide range of inventions, from small workshop tools to the engines of the lightning trains themselves.

The trains are the most visible application of this technology. Their rods pull energy continuously while in motion, maintaining speed through a constant feed of captured sparks. A train that stops pulling sparks is a train that is slowing down.

## Ashspire

Some distance from Trademoor sits [Ashspire](/expansion/locations/thren/ashspire), the empire's great university town, connected to the capital by lightning train. Ashspire contains only its university buildings, a small market, and accommodation, and relies heavily on shipments from Trademoor to keep its stalls stocked.
