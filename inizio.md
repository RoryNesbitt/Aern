# Inizio

Inizio is the home of the gods and the afterlife of mortals. Its many inner layers are home to the [Estasi](/deities/estasi), the [Inestasi](/deities/inestasi), and the Ancients, as well as the souls of every person who has ever died on Aern.

Souls entering Inizio must make the journey through Limbo to the Underworld, or risk losing their minds entirely and becoming lost. Everything that exists in Inizio, outside of the Underworld, is formed from these mindless lost souls — from the trees of Limbo's forest to the demons created by the Inestasi. A person who stays too long in one spot will begin to see faces pressing out from the surfaces around them, the lost trying to escape, drawn toward the warmth of a living mind.

If a person encounters one of the Ancients on their journey through Inizio, they may lose their mind simply from looking upon it.

## Outer Inizio

To arrive in Inizio without being anchored to one of its layers is to find yourself in none of them. Outer Inizio is a chaotic, churning environment where elements of all other layers spill in without warning and vanish just as suddenly — a patch of Limbo's colourless forest appearing beside a gout of hellstone, then gone. There are native Inizian species here that have learned to exert some control over this chaos, but for a traveller it is deeply disorienting and dangerous.

## Limbo

Limbo is the first layer encountered by souls on their way to the Underworld, and it can appear differently to different people. Most will find themselves in the Forest of Suolo — a desolate, colourless woodland with little foliage, where the silence is broken only by the distant sound of things that were once people, wandering without purpose.

[Suolo](/deities/estasi#suolo) guides souls through Limbo toward the Underworld. Those who are not guided, or who lose their way, risk becoming part of the landscape.

## The Underworld

The Underworld provides protection against the maddening effects of Inizio, including the influence of the Ancients. It is a sanctuary to any soul that reaches it, regardless of the life they led — though the judgements of [Cordanna](/deities/estasi#cordanna) may determine how far within a soul is permitted to go.

### The Endless Fields

The main expanse of the Underworld, connected to Limbo at its great gate. Souls that remain here are protected from the corruption of Inizio, but the endlessness of death eventually erodes their identity. They are safe, but they are fading.

### The Gardens

Where most souls ultimately come to rest. Only those who led a truly evil life are turned away. The Gardens are brighter and more colourful than the Fields, and they impart a deep sense of peace in those who dwell within them. They are, by all accounts, a very pleasant place to spend an eternity.

### The City of Eterna

Reserved for the exceptional few — those whose lives on Aern were marked by deeds great enough to earn a place beyond the Gardens. Eterna has its own heavenly gardens and provides access to the palace of the gods, where a soul may occasionally meet with one of the Estasi.

Residents of Eterna are permitted to cross into the Gardens to spend time with souls who did not share their distinction in life. At the centre of Eterna, within the palace grounds, stands the [Tree of Life](tree-of-life) — a tree that exists across the entire multiverse. Those with sufficient skill can use it to travel between worlds. It is also the means by which a soul granted a second life returns to the material plane.

## Inferno

Contrary to most religious belief, Inferno does not exist to punish the dead — though some [Inestasi](/deities/inestasi) do take a certain enjoyment in doing so when the opportunity arises. It is, more accurately, a collection of personal kingdoms carved out by the [Inestasi](/deities/inestasi), each one shaped entirely to the tastes of the god who rules it.

A few of these layers serve a purpose in the creation of demon armies. Most exist purely for the entertainment of their ruler.

### The Fields of War
*Ruler: [Furia](/deities/inestasi#furia)*

Like the Underworld, the first layer of Inferno is a seemingly endless field. Unlike the Underworld, these fields are locked in constant battle. The lesser Inestasi and any mortals who find themselves here — living or dead — are driven to violent rage simply by the act of being present. There is no peace in the Fields of War, only the next fight.

### Labirinto
*Ruler: [Abisso Notte](/deities/inestasi#abisso-notte)*

The shifting maze of Labirinto is the life's work of [Abinot](/deities/inestasi#abisso-notte), the Great Architect. There are many ways in. There is only one way out — the tower at the maze's heart, where Abisso Notte watches over the whole of his creation. To traverse Labirinto is to accept that it will change around you, and to find a way through regardless.

### The Dark Library
*Ruler: [Infausto](/deities/inestasi#infausto)*

The Dark Library holds copies of every book that has ever existed — full shelves, piles more than five metres high, and loose pages strewn across every surface. Unlike the knowledge that flows freely to Dati's study, nothing here arrives on its own. Every piece of it has been found, retrieved, and written down by [Faust](/deities/inestasi#infausto) or one of his underlings. Books that originate here can become powerful artefacts if they find their way to Aern.

### Caer Strazio
*Ruler: [Sventura](/deities/inestasi#sventura)*

At the astral centre of all Inferno lies the expansive castle Caer Strazio, home of [Sventura](/deities/inestasi#sventura), the self-proclaimed head of the Inestasi. Caer Strazio serves as the meeting place for the Inestasi, though they rarely agree long enough to actually organise one. Its war room contains a living map of Aern. Throughout its halls, doorways open into every other layer of Inferno.

### The Lost Hand
*Ruler: [Ladro](/deities/inestasi#ladro)*

The most frequently visited layer of Inferno. The Lost Hand is open to thieves who have earned [Ladro's](/deities/inestasi#ladro) respect, and it has a habit of appearing where it shouldn't — an unexpected doorway leading somewhere it clearly should not go. Some permanent portals do exist for those who know where to look.

Within, the inn appears entirely ordinary. A traveller might not even realise they have left Aern — until they look out of the windows, where the hellish landscape of Inferno stretches in every direction. The shutters are always closed for this reason.

Opposite the bar is the entry door, surrounded by an unusual arcane contraption. Each time it is opened the many gears burst into life, selecting the combination of runes that will return the person to wherever they came from. On the side wall is a large red door marked *Staff Only* in hundreds of different languages. It leads to Caer Strazio.

### The Demonweb
*Ruler: [Ammazzare](/deities/inestasi#ammazzare)*

Vast silk webs stretch across this entire layer of Inferno, connecting floating platforms of hellstone, each with its own gravitational orientation. The web belongs to [Ammazzare](/deities/inestasi#ammazzare), the Spider Queen, and traversing it is a sure way to reveal your location to her. The alternative — leaping between the closer rocks — risks falling into Outer Inizio or landing in an entirely different layer of Inferno. Each platform comes with its own demons and its own dangers.

Caught in the webbing are the silk cocoons of people taken through this layer's portals to the lower chasms of the [Vile Caves](/vile-caves) on Aern. One of the platforms has been claimed by corrupted automatons from the Azzurium Age research party, who have built themselves an outpost not unlike those found in the caves below.

### Nulla
*Ruler: [Maligno](/deities/inestasi#maligno)*

Nulla is a plane of nothing. A layer of Inferno that holds no life, no sound, no movement — only the slow dissolution of anything sent into it. Nothing other than an Inizian can survive here; anything else will gradually degrade and disappear. A small number of scholars who know of Nulla's existence have theorised that its destructive power could be harnessed within an arcane crystal. Everyone who has attempted it has died doing so.

[Maligno](/deities/inestasi#maligno) created Nulla because it is the only place he has ever felt at peace.
